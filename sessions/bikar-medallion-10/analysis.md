# Analysis — bikar-medallion-10

## Reference Image
10-fold medallion with satellite rosettes and strapwork bands.

## Symmetry
- **Order:** 10-fold rotational
- **Star notation:** {10/4} primary star
- **Confirmed by:** 10 outer scallops, 10 satellite rosettes, 10-pointed central star

## Concentric Zones (center → edge)
1. **Zone 0 (center):** Dark navy 10-pointed star, decagonal center void
2. **Zone 1 (inner kites):** 10 kite faces in medium blue + turquoise triangular accents
3. **Zone 2 (interstitial):** Kites + rhombi in mixed blues (navy, cobalt, periwinkle)
4. **Zone 3 (satellites):** 10 smaller 10-pointed satellite rosettes, each with its own star center

## Strapwork
- **Present:** Yes — white interlaced bands throughout
- **Width:** ~5-8% of pattern radius
- **Color:** White (#FFFFFF)
- **Weaving:** Alternating over/under at all crossings

## Face Types
- Triangles (3-sided) — abundant, small
- Kites (4-sided) — medium, radial orientation
- Rhombi (4-sided) — interstitial zone
- Pentagons (5-sided) — satellite rosettes
- Decagons (10-sided) — star and satellite centers

## Construction Hypothesis
- Central circle C0 divided into 10
- `connect every 4` for primary {10/4} star
- `connect every 2` for secondary connections
- `repeat at C0 depth 2` for satellite rosettes at ring-1 and ring-2 positions
- `strapwork width 8 crossing alternating` for white interlaced bands
- Ring-based + face-type-based coloring using the blue palette
